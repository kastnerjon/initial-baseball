# Monetization Strategy

## Principle

Initial Baseball should be a free social game first. Do not charge users before the core loop proves retention.

## Alpha

- Free.
- No ads.
- No subscriptions.
- No paid leagues.
- No paid app download.

Alpha goal is product proof, not revenue.

## Future monetization paths

| Path | Fit | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Pro subscription | High | Unlimited active games, advanced settings, advanced stats, no ads. |
| Light ads | Medium | Only between natural breaks; never mid-at-bat. |
| Private leagues | Very high | Best long-term wedge. Commissioner tools, season history, playoffs. |
| Cosmetics | Medium | Avatars, badges, profile themes. |
| Paid download | Low | Hurts social spread. Avoid. |
| Pay-to-win | Terrible | Never sell extra guesses, better hints, or gameplay advantage. |

## Likely paid tiers later

### Free

- Friend games.
- Random opponent games.
- Basic settings.
- Basic records.
- Limited active games if needed.
- Light ads if introduced later.

### Pro

- Unlimited active games.
- Advanced hint types.
- Advanced stats picker.
- Deeper records/head-to-head history.
- No ads.
- Profile cosmetics.

### Private League upgrades

- Larger leagues.
- Commissioner tools.
- Seasons/playoffs.
- League history.
- Custom league settings.

## Store commissions

Do not design around avoiding app-store commission early. If digital features unlock inside the mobile app, assume Apple/Google in-app purchase rules may apply. Ads and sponsorships are separate monetization channels, but subscriptions/unlocks should be planned as IAP unless reviewed otherwise.
